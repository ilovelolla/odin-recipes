Bulalo

Warm your soul with a bowl of Bulalo — a rich and comforting Filipino beef soup made with tender shank and bone marrow. Slow-cooked to perfection, this dish is infused with natural beef flavors and vegetables, making it a perfect rainy-day or weekend family meal. Sip the flavorful broth, enjoy the fall-off-the-bone beef, and savor every spoonful.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef shank (with bone marrow)
  • 1 medium onion (quartered)
  • 1 tbsp whole peppercorns
  • 1–2 tbsp fish sauce or salt (to taste)
  • 2 cobs corn (cut into 3 pieces each)
  • 1 small bundle of pechay or cabbage
  • 3 medium potatoes (peeled, quartered)
  • 1 liter water (add more as needed)
  • Green onions (optional, for garnish)

How to Cook Bulalo

  1. In a large pot, boil beef shanks in water.
  2. Skim off scum and fat that rise to the top.
  3. Add onions and peppercorns; simmer for 2–3 hours until beef is tender.
  4. Add corn and potatoes; cook until tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce or salt.
  6. Add pechay or cabbage last; simmer for 2–3 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with rice and dipping sauce (fish sauce with calamansi or chili).
